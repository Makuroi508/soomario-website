THE STRUCTURAL EDGE

Token unlocks create
predictable selling pressure

The thesis is simple. When tokens unlock — to teams, to investors, to ecosystems — recipients sell some portion of their allocation. That sell pressure is structurally bearish for any project whose existing holders aren't aggressively absorbing it. Most of the market doesn't trade unlocks systematically. Either they ignore them entirely, or they trade them anecdotally — "XYZ has a big unlock next week, let's short it" — without statistical discipline.

Alpha treats unlocks as a primary signal with confirmation requirements. The engine doesn't short every upcoming unlock — most of them are already priced in. It shorts only the unlocks that coincide with technical, funding, and liquidation conditions suggesting the market is unprepared for the supply increase. That filtering is what turns publicly available data into a tradable edge.

Across roughly 17 actively tracked unlock candidates at any given time, the engine typically opens 2–4 short positions per scoring cycle. The selectivity is the point.

SIGNAL ARCHITECTURE

Four independent signals.
Composite, not single-shot.

Every four hours, Alpha re-scans the universe and produces a composite score for each asset across four independent signal dimensions. A high score on any single signal isn't enough — the engine demands corroboration before deploying capital.

SIGNAL 1 · DOMINANT
Unlock Proximity

A curated database of upcoming token unlock events. Candidates are weighted by how close the unlock is, what type of recipient is unlocking (team and investor unlocks score higher than ecosystem ones), and how large the unlock is relative to circulating supply.

SIGNAL 2
Technical Overextension

Multi-hour candle data identifies assets that have rallied beyond reasonable mean-reversion bounds. The kind of overbought setup where unlock-driven selling has somewhere to land. A high reading here adds weight to any unlock candidate already in the queue.

SIGNAL 3
Funding & Crowding

Perpetual funding rates reveal positioning directly. When longs are paying shorts to hold the trade, the position pays you to wait — every funding period. Structurally favorable conditions for the short side.

SIGNAL 4
Liquidation Asymmetry

The open interest book reveals where liquidation cascades would fire. An asset with heavy long-side liquidation potential below current price is structurally fragile — any dip can trigger forced selling that accelerates the move.

THE CONFIRMATION RULE

The composite score weights unlock proximity heavily, but requires corroboration from at least one secondary signal. A high score alone isn't enough — the engine demands either a near-term unlock or strong technical overextension before opening a position. The exact weights and thresholds are calibrated against historical data and not published.

SPREAD CONSTRUCTION

HYPE long anchor.

LONG SIDE
HYPE Anchor

The long side holds a position in HYPE, sized to a fixed pool target with moderate leverage. Established through layered DCA — initial seed at opening, with reserve capital deployed if price tests lower zones. Once profitable, the position transitions through a trail-and-defend lifecycle: peaks tracked, partial exits trim as profit grows, buy-back marker re-enters opportunistically if a defended floor holds.

HYPE is the anchor because Hyperliquid is, by every available metric, the strongest standalone perpetual exchange. Real revenue. Organic user growth. Own L1. No dependence on third-party liquidity. In a crash, HYPE tends to outperform the long tail of speculative alts. In a rally, it participates. As an anchor for a spread strategy, it's nearly ideal.

SHORT SIDE
Weak Altcoin Sleeve

The short side operates as a slot-based sleeve. A small fixed number of slots are available; each represents a fraction of equity and gets deployed when the engine identifies a qualifying candidate. New positions enter in two stages: a modest probe when the candidate first clears threshold, then a larger conviction add when either the unlock catalyst is imminent or price has rallied further into overextension.

The conviction add has a rejection condition: if price has rallied too far past the initial probe, the second tranche is canceled. Excessive pump suggests the short thesis may be breaking. Better to leave the position small than to add aggressively into a face-rip.

LEVERAGE CAP

All shorts use leverage capped at 5×. This cap is deliberate. Earlier iterations used higher leverage and learned, expensively, that a tighter stop band is not worth the capital efficiency. At 5×, an 8% adverse move triggers exchange-side stop. At 10×, a 4% adverse move does the same — and 4% is inside the noise floor. The 8% band is approximately the volatility budget the strategy needs to ride out normal market noise.

RISK MANAGEMENT

Stops on the exchange.
Not on the bot.

The most consequential design choice in Alpha: every position has its stop placed directly on Hyperliquid at the moment of entry. Stops fire whether the bot is alive, reachable, rate-limited, or restarting.

Exchange-Side Stops

A reduce-only stop-market trigger placed directly on Hyperliquid at entry, set at −40% uplRatio (the position has lost 40% of its margin). At 5× leverage that's an 8% adverse price move; at 3× it's 13.3%. Wide on purpose — false stops cost the position and the future opportunity.

Bot-Side Redundancy

Independent of the exchange-side stop, the bot polls every position every five minutes for the same threshold. If the exchange order somehow fails to fire, the bot-side path closes the position via market order. Two layers, same target.

Reconcile Guard

Any state change that would close real positions requires a fresh, force-refreshed read from the exchange to confirm. If the exchange contradicts the close decision, the close is aborted entirely. Invisible during normal operation; exists for the day when something unexpected happens.

Staged Take-Profit

Profitable shorts exit in tranches rather than all at once. Partial profit at successive drawdown levels, with a residual position kept open to capture extended downtrends. Front-load risk reduction, back-load potential.

Event-Driven State

Steady-state monitoring runs entirely off CEX data feeds, which are higher-throughput and free. Hyperliquid state queries happen only when needed for a decision. Earlier versions polled state aggressively and starved themselves of execution windows; this design fixes that.

Spread Construction

Alpha is not a market-direction bet. The HYPE long + weak altcoin shorts construction profits when high-quality assets outperform low-quality ones — a relationship that holds in most market conditions, including drawdowns where quality flight is most pronounced.

EDGE THESIS

Why this works
when others don't

Public data, rare systematic application

Token unlock schedules are on every project's tokenomics page and aggregated by DeFiLlama. But trading them at scale, with discipline, against a multi-signal validation framework — that's rare. Most unlock-trading is anecdotal or reactive. Alpha is neither.

Multi-signal confirmation reduces false starts

The engine doesn't short anything based on a single indicator. It demands agreement from at least two independent sources before deploying capital. This filters out the unlocks that are already priced in — which is most of them.

Stops don't depend on bot uptime

Most automated strategies have a single point of failure: if the bot crashes or gets rate-limited, positions run unmanaged. Alpha's stops fire on the exchange's matching engine whether the bot is alive or not.

Spread construction limits directional risk

Alpha profits when high-quality assets outperform low-quality ones — a relationship that holds in most market conditions. Particularly pronounced in drawdowns where flight-to-quality is most acute.

Discipline beats novelty

Average in, average out. Don't try to time tops. Take partial profit. Leave runners. None of these are novel ideas. Few apply them with discipline across hundreds of assets, 24 hours a day.

HONEST LIMITS

What Alpha isn't

Not a high-frequency strategy

The pipeline scans every four hours. Performance is measured by months, not minutes. If you're looking for a strategy that fires dozens of trades per day, this isn't it.

Not a directional macro bet

Alpha doesn't try to call market tops or bottoms. It exploits a specific structural inefficiency. In a generalized risk-on rally where every asset moves up together, the short sleeve gives back gains that the long anchor captures — and that's by design.

Not capacity-unlimited

As assets under management grow, the slot-based approach naturally constrains size. There's a ceiling, and it's lower than most discretionary strategies. Alpha is not designed to absorb unlimited capital.

Not foolproof

Drawdowns happen. Stops get hit. Some unlock theses don't play out — sometimes the recipient holds, sometimes a buyer absorbs, sometimes the news cycle drives a face-rip rally that triggers the stop before the unlock catalyst even arrives. The edge is statistical, not deterministic.
