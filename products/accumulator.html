SIGNAL LOGIC

Three indicators.
All must agree.

Any single indicator throws too many false positives. Requiring confluence across three independent measures cuts the noise and drives the signal quality up.

INDICATOR 1
RSI (Relative Strength)

Momentum oscillator that measures how oversold an asset has become. The Accumulator uses fast-reacting RSI to catch short-term capitulation — the moment when sellers have pushed an asset below its fair value before dip-buyers step in.

INDICATOR 2
Price vs Moving Average

How far below trend is the current price? The Accumulator measures each asset against its long-term moving average. Deeper discounts produce stronger signals — and bigger buys.

INDICATOR 3
Volatility Z-Score (crypto)

For BTC and SOL, a statistical lookback measures how unusual the current move is relative to recent history. Rare capitulation events — the kind that mark local bottoms — show up here as extreme negative readings. Exactly when you want to be a buyer.

THE COMPOSITE SCORE

Each indicator's reading gets normalized and combined into a single signal-strength score between 1× and 5×. Stocks and crypto carry their own tuned weightings — because what counts as "extreme" for BTC is not the same as for NVDA. A 1× signal fires your baseline daily allocation; a 5× signal fires five times that. Deeper capitulation deserves bigger buys.

THE BUDGET FORMULA

How your buy amounts
get calculated

The math is deliberately simple. You tell the bot your monthly budget — say $500. It divides that across the month and across the signal-strength multipliers so you always have capital available when the best opportunities show up.

THE FORMULA
safe_daily_rate = monthly_budget / (30 × avg_multiplier)
signal_buy = safe_daily_rate × signal_strength

The average multiplier is assumed at 2.5×. So for a $500/month budget: $500 ÷ (30 × 2.5) = $6.67 safe daily rate. A typical signal of 2.5× strength sends you $16.67. A maximum 5× signal sends you $33.33. If the market crashes and multiple 5× signals fire on the same day, you might buy $100+ in a single day — but the math ensures you won't run out of capital before month-end even in extreme scenarios.

FAQ

Straightforward answers

Why are the metrics so good? Should I be skeptical?

Healthy skepticism is the right instinct. The numbers you see are real trades with real prices since February 2026 — but they cover a narrow window, 109 signals, and most of them fired during an AI sector uptrend. A 93% win rate over 2.5 months is not a sustainable baseline forecast; it will regress as the sample grows and the market cycles through less favorable conditions. What you're seeing is "the best plausible case" for this strategy, not the expected case. What should hold up over longer timeframes is the structural edge: buying oversold quality assets at scaled sizes tends to outperform blind DCA. The magnitude of that outperformance will vary.

What happens during a market crash?

A broad crash triggers multiple 5× maximum-strength signals across many assets on the same day. The budget math is specifically designed for this — your safe daily rate is calculated so that even worst-case crash allocation doesn't drain your monthly budget too fast. In practice you'll buy aggressively on the way down, which is what you want. The bot holds no position management on exits; it's a pure accumulation system, so "what if prices keep falling?" just means you keep getting more attractive entry signals until the crash ends.

What if I miss a signal?

Nothing breaks. Signals are entry opportunities, not deadlines. If you miss one, the next one will fire when conditions are right again — often within days. The Telegram alerts include a timestamp so you can check how fresh a signal is when you do see it. Missing signals hurts your averaging quality marginally, but the system is robust to imperfect execution.

Do I have to execute automatically?

No — and you can't. The Accumulator is signals-only. Every buy is a manual action you take on your own broker or exchange. This is intentional: it keeps your funds in your own accounts, removes any custody risk on our end, and works regardless of which broker or exchange you use. It also means you can skip signals when you have good reason to, which is a feature, not a bug.

What exchanges and brokers are supported?

Any broker that lets you buy US equities and any exchange that lets you buy BTC and SOL. Because execution is manual, there's no integration requirement. Common setups: Robinhood, Fidelity, Charles Schwab, Interactive Brokers for stocks; Coinbase, Kraken, Binance for crypto. The signals tell you what and how much — you pick where.

What if I'm in a different time zone?

Signal timestamps are in UTC by default. In your dashboard settings you can set your preferred display timezone and the bot will show all times in your local zone. Most signals fire during US market hours for stocks (which means late-night and early-morning depending on where you are), and 24/7 for crypto. Telegram will buzz when they fire — you can execute whenever you next open your broker.
