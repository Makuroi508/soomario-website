THE EDGE

Aphelion — the orbital extreme

In orbital mechanics, aphelion denotes the point in an elliptical orbit farthest from the sun — where an object moves slowest, where gravity has the most work to do to pull it back. The metaphor is direct: when a stock trades furthest from its mean, the restorative force is mechanical, and the slow-moving opportunity is largest.

Three observations underpin the strategy. Short-horizon mean reversion in liquid US large-caps is empirically persistent — RSI(2) readings below 30 cluster around local minima with statistical regularity, even controlling for trend regime. Conventional dip-buying defaults to fixed or volatility-scaled sizing, which under-allocates to the rare conditions that justify the largest commitment. And exit asymmetry matters more than entry precision — a position closed at first sign of profit captures less than the same position trailed loosely through a multi-week recovery. Aphelion's design follows from those three.

WHAT YOU GET

One entry. One exit.

Configuration K is intentionally minimal. No layered take-profits, no defensive averaging-down, no time-based exits. Every additional mechanism was tested in the backtest sweep and rejected on Monte Carlo evidence.

Composite oversold signal

RSI(2) contribution (60% weight) measures short-horizon overshoots. MA(300) distance (40%) weights dips below the long-term trend more aggressively than dips within it. A signal fires when composite strength clears 30 on a 0–100 scale — below that threshold, the dip is treated as noise. Position size scales 1.0× to 5.0× the per-asset daily commitment based on signal strength.

Days-since-fire accumulation

Per-asset sizing scales with days since the last signal fire — so the first signal after a long dry spell carries more notional than a signal in a cluster. This keeps average monthly deployment near a target budget while allowing burst allocation at signal extremes. Per-trade hard cap at 1% × leverage prevents over-concentration even at maximum multiplier.

Recycling tail trail

The position's only exit. Trail arms when price climbs 50% above the cycle anchor. Trailing stop sits 25% below the highest price seen. On stop hit, 50% of position closes, the anchor advances to the trim price, the trail re-arms — and the remaining 50% participates in continued upside. Daily evaluation at 23:00 UTC. Average entry is never modified.

Why this universe, why isolated margin

2× isolated leverage was chosen over the originally specified 3× after Monte Carlo showed meaningfully lower catastrophic-drawdown probability at negligible expected-return cost — approximately 3 percentage points of CAGR given up for a 12-percentage-point reduction in the probability of greater-than-70% drawdown. Isolated margin caps single-asset blowup at its own allocated margin and prevents cross-contamination across the basket. Capital efficiency takes a small hit; risk isolation is the structural payoff.

A heavy-tailed, asymmetric distribution

The Monte Carlo distribution is intentionally asymmetric. Median outcomes are strongly positive, but the left tail extends to near-total loss in roughly 5% of paths — and the probability of experiencing a greater-than-70% unrealized drawdown at some point in the horizon is 77%. This is the structural cost of geometric compounding with leverage on a basket that can experience correlated drawdowns. Aphelion is not designed for capital preservation in adverse regimes; it is designed to capture geometrically compounding returns across a typical range of conditions, with kill switches and isolated margin bounding (not eliminating) left-tail risk.

HONEST LIMITS

What Aphelion isn't

Not designed for capital preservation

The strategy uses leverage and concentrated single-asset positions to capture geometric compounding. Drawdowns of 70% or more are within design tolerance and appear in 77% of Monte Carlo paths at some point in the horizon. Investors who cannot tolerate that level of mark-to-market volatility should not participate, even if they're comfortable with the long-run expectation.

Liquidations are within design tolerance

Isolated margin caps single-asset liquidation to its own allocated capital — it does not prevent liquidations. The 2020–2026 backtest shows 17 per-asset liquidations across the simulation period. A 30-day post-liquidation cooldown prevents sequential re-entry during prolonged drawdown regimes, but the underlying event still costs the affected position's allocated margin.

Heavy tails are real

Approximately 5% of Monte Carlo paths end below 1% of starting equity. This is the structural cost of geometric compounding with leverage on a basket that can experience correlated drawdowns. Kill switches halt new entries at 20% daily loss or 50% cumulative unrealized drawdown, but the trail keeps managing existing positions and capital already at risk continues to be at risk.

Backtest regime is predominantly bullish

The 2020–2026 backtest period includes the COVID dislocation and one significant correction but is otherwise dominated by bull-market regime. Strategy performance in a prolonged bear market is largely untested in live conditions. The recycling-tail design captures recoveries; in a regime where recoveries do not arrive, that capture mechanism produces fewer harvests and smaller trims.
