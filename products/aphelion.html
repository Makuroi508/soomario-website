SIGNAL LOGIC

Three signals.
All must align.

The engine pulls daily OHLCV for 15 assets and computes three independent indicators. Only when all three agree does the strength score cross threshold and a position open. On most days, zero positions open.

SIGNAL 1
RSI Oversold Detection

A fast-reacting Relative Strength Index identifies short-term capitulation moves — the kind of price action that emotionally looks terrible on the chart but historically marks reversal points in mean-reverting markets. Stocks and crypto carry their own oversold thresholds.

SIGNAL 2
Volume Capitulation

A volume Z-score that measures up-volume against down-volume separately, flagging candles where forced selling has dominated. This is the difference between "drifting lower" and "panic-selling lower" — Aphelion only acts on the latter.

SIGNAL 3
Trend Extension

Price extension below the long-period moving average — how stretched is the asset relative to its own trend? Combined with the other two signals, this filters out healthy pullbacks and keeps only the genuine dislocations where buyers historically get paid.

THE STRENGTH SCORE

The three signals combine into a weighted strength score from 0 to 100. Only crossings of a minimum threshold trigger a signal; everything else is logged and skipped. The exact threshold and weights are calibrated against backtest data and not published.

POSITION CONSTRUCTION

Layered entry.
Layered defense. Layered exits.

STEP 1
Primary Entry

Each confirmed signal opens a long position at 2× isolated leverage. Sizing follows an accumulator formula that scales by signal multiplier and days since the asset last received a signal. Multiple signals in the same day stack additively up to a daily cap.

STEP 2
Defense Layer

As soon as a position opens, the engine places a passive limit BUY order on the exchange book above the position's calculated liquidation price. If price ever drops to that trigger, the order fills automatically — no slippage, no bot dependency, no polling lag. The position averages down to a new lower basis and the take-profit ladder resets.

STEP 3
Layered Exit

Three predetermined take-profit levels close fixed fractions of the original position size as price moves up. The remainder rides on a daily-evaluated trailing stop that arms once profitable and tracks the all-time peak. Multi-layer exits capture more of the outcome distribution than any single exit decision could.

In the 6-year backtest, defense fired on roughly 2% of position lifecycles — most positions exit via take-profit or trail without ever needing it. The defense layer's value is in the small fraction of cases where it does fire — precisely when the position would otherwise have been liquidated.

RISK CONTROLS

Six layers of protection

Pre-placed Limit Defense

A resting limit order on the exchange book above each position's liquidation price. Fills with maker pricing and zero slippage. Doesn't depend on the bot being online.

5-Minute Reconciliation

Every five minutes, the engine queries the exchange for ground-truth position state and reconciles against internal records. External closes are detected. Defense fills are reconstructed. State drift never compounds.

Account-Level Kill Switch

Halts all new entries if intraday losses exceed a threshold or total unrealized PnL drops below a floor. Sticky — once tripped, requires explicit operator reset before new signals are accepted.

Daily & Per-Trade Caps

A per-trade notional cap and a daily total cap apply on top of position-level controls. Multiple signals in a single day cannot collectively breach the daily limit regardless of strength scores.

2× Leverage Ceiling

Higher leverage produced higher returns but markedly more liquidations across the 6-year window. At 2×, the all-15 universe backtest produced zero liquidations across roughly 1,000 position lifecycles. Survival is the constraint.

Long-Only by Construction

Aphelion does not short. The strategy is a long-bias accumulator. In a generalized sell-off where every asset drops together, it accumulates more aggressively — that's the design and the risk.

HONEST LIMITS

What Aphelion isn't

Not a market-timing strategy

The engine doesn't predict tops or bottoms. It buys oversold conditions and holds through volatility. Long stretches of grinding sideways action will see Aphelion accumulate slowly without exciting moves.

Not high-frequency

Most days, zero signals fire. Most weeks, fewer than three new positions open. The selectivity is the point, not a bug.

Not infinite capacity

At current sizing, Aphelion operates well under any individual asset's order book depth. At 100× larger AUM, the strategy would either need to reduce per-trade sizes proportionally or accept market impact on entries. Capacity is real and finite.

Not a replacement for diversification

Long-bias on 15 risk assets with 2× leverage is correlated to broad equity and crypto risk. In a generalized sell-off where every asset moves down together, Aphelion accumulates more aggressively. That's the design, but it concentrates exposure to systemic moves.

Not foolproof

Drawdowns happen. Defense limits get filled. Some positions trail-out at small losses. The edge is statistical, not deterministic.
