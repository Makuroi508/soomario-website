THE THESIS

Most Strategies Fail Three Ways

Most retail algorithmic strategies fail for one of three reasons: they fight cyclicality with purely technical signals, they anchor to fundamentals without regard to entry timing, or they concentrate risk in a single asset class and blow up when the regime shifts.

Rotation is built to avoid all three. Rather than pick one edge and hope it persists, the strategy runs three sleeves with structurally different return sources. The stock sleeve compounds through secular growth. The crypto core captures digital-asset asymmetry while dollar-cost-averaging through volatility. The short sleeve realizes gains when supply dilution hits oversold AI-era cryptos — often during the same periods when the long sleeves are in drawdown.

The resulting equity curve is not dominated by any single market regime. Each sleeve has its own signals, its own cadence, and its own risk controls. They share only the execution venue.

SLEEVE 1 DETAIL

Fastest Horse Scoring

The highest-return equity allocation over 3-to-5 years is not the stock with the best recent performance or the cheapest multiple. It's the stock with the largest risk-adjusted upside to a credible long-horizon target. Score it, rank it, rotate into the top five, reassess weekly.

LAYER 1 · 25 POINTS
Consensus Upside

Wall Street 12-month price target versus current price. A sanity check against sell-side positioning. Sourced from StockAnalysis, MarketBeat, TipRanks, Public.com, Benzinga with yfinance primary + static fallback.

LAYER 2 · 50 POINTS
Visionary Upside

Long-horizon conviction targets from ARK Research, InvestAnswers theses, and Jon Erlichman profit-growth aggregates. Dominates the score because visionary targets are the hardest to fake — they change slowly and reflect genuine analyst thesis conviction rather than news cycles.

LAYER 3 · 25 POINTS
Technical Momentum

4H RSI, Supertrend, and position relative to MA-50 — all on Hyperliquid candles. Technicals confirm or veto the thesis; they don't drive the pick. The rotation is not a momentum chase.

CHURN CONTROL

An 8-point hysteresis prevents weekly thrashing. A held stock must be beaten by ≥8 points before rotation; a new candidate must exceed the lowest held score by ≥8 points to enter. When scores cluster tightly, positions stay stable.

ZONE MECHANICS

Every 4 hours, a zone aggregator computes volume-weighted Long Max Pain and Short Max Pain across Binance, Bybit, and OKX. Between those two levels, five DCA entries are interpolated with a 6% ATR multiplier. L1 seeds near current price, L2 sits at the Long MP magnet itself, L3–L5 extend deeper for increasingly rare conditions. The OI ratio (long vs short open interest balance) serves as a secondary regime signal — short-heavy order books tend to revert upward as shorts cover.

SLEEVE 3 DETAIL

Hedged Shorts · 10-Level TP Grid

Shorting is the most dangerous sleeve — funding drains returns, squeezes force liquidation, and the asymmetry of the trade punishes overconviction. Rotation treats shorts as tactical: capped at 10% of capital, capped at 4 simultaneous positions, validated through four gates before entry, exited through a back-loaded grid that banks partial profits fast.

Four-Gate Validator

GATE 1

RSI divergence or overbought exhaustion signal.

GATE 2

90-day Z-Score indicating overextension.

GATE 3

Momentum exhaustion check (diminishing up-day returns).

GATE 4

Confluence: at least two independent weakness signals agree.

The 10-Level Back-Loaded TP Grid

RISK DISCLOSURE

Regime risk. The scoring engine assumes visionary targets from ARK and similar analysts remain informative. A regime where these targets lose credibility (e.g., prolonged AI capex contraction) degrades the signal across multiple sleeves simultaneously.

Correlation risk. Stocks, crypto longs, and crypto shorts all benefit from risk-on environments. In a coordinated selloff, only the short sleeve provides genuine diversification — and shorts are capped at 10%, limiting hedge capacity.

Execution risk. Hyperliquid is a single venue. Prolonged HL outages freeze everything. Stock perps are HL-only, so no fallback venue for equity trades exists today.

Liquidity risk. HL stock perps have thinner books than cash equities. Slippage on large orders can erode the DCA edge.

Short squeeze risk. The short sleeve runs 5× leverage. A coordinated squeeze on any of the four active shorts could force liquidation before the grid trims meaningfully. The 10% cap and 4-position limit bound the damage.

Paper vs live. Paper trading doesn't model slippage, order queue behavior, or the psychology of real capital. Live results will differ from paper — sometimes materially. Nothing in this document constitutes investment advice.
