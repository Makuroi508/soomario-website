THE STRUCTURAL EDGE

Volatility Is Overpriced.
Calls Sellers Get Paid for It.

Crypto options markets consistently price implied volatility above realized volatility, especially during fear-driven regimes. Most participants either avoid the strategy or sell calls too aggressively. Mechanical execution closes both gaps.

Implied volatility — what the market expects — consistently exceeds realized volatility, the actual move that ends up happening. That gap exists in every asset class but is wider and more persistent in crypto. When the Fear & Greed index drops below 25, premiums inflate as hedgers pay up for protection — and the data is fully public. Anyone can compare IV to RV on Deribit's analytics page.

What's uncommon is selling into that gap systematically across multiple assets with disciplined strike selection. Most retail participants either: pick strikes too close to spot in pursuit of premium and get assigned out of their coins; manage the rolls and expirations manually and inevitably miss cycles; or avoid options entirely because the operational complexity feels insurmountable. The strategy itself isn't novel — call writing has been written about for fifty years. The mechanical, multi-asset, around-the-clock application is what's rare.

TIMING ENGINE

Five Signals.
All Must Agree.

Most losses in call-writing strategies don't come from the strategy itself — they come from selling at the wrong time. The timing engine requires five independent signals to agree before placing a SELL order.

SIGNAL 1
Conviction Scoring

RSI and price Z-score combined to assess whether the coin is in a favorable range for selling calls. The ideal setup: trading in the upper portion of recent range, where premiums are rich but not so elevated that breakout is imminent.

SIGNAL 2
Supertrend Direction

Acts as a directional gate. When the trend is bearish, the engine is more willing to sell calls because the trend itself caps upside. A fresh bullish flip triggers an explicit AVOID flag.

SIGNAL 3
Farmability

Measures how well a coin trades within a defined range. Coins with tight, aligned zones across timeframes score higher because their behavior is more predictable. Coins making new highs with no resistance overhead score lower.

SIGNAL 4
Zone Proximity

Uses the same liquidation-cascade data as Soomario Zones. Strikes are placed above the cascade zones plus a volatility buffer — beyond the price levels where forced liquidations would accelerate a rally.

SIGNAL 5
Regime Read

Fear & Greed index and broader market context. Premiums inflate during fear regimes — that's exactly when call sellers earn the most, but only if the underlying isn't already capitulating into a freefall.

CONFIDENCE THRESHOLD

Each signal contributes to a composite confidence score from 0 to 100. Only scores above the minimum threshold trigger SELL NOW; everything below produces WAIT. Across the 7 monitored coins, the engine typically issues 2–4 SELL signals per scan cycle. The remaining receive WAIT. Selectivity is the point — the strategy only acts when timing, trend, range structure, zone placement, and regime all agree.

HOW POSITIONS ARE BUILT

Wide Strikes.
Mechanical Rolls.

STRIKE SELECTION
7-12% OTM Sweet Spot

Strikes are selected in the 7–12% OTM range — far enough from spot to provide buffer against crypto volatility, close enough to collect meaningful premium. Strikes below 5% OTM are hard-filtered, regardless of premium.

SIZING
Fully Covered, Always

Position size matches the account's holdings in each coin. If the account holds 100 BTC, the engine sells calls on 100 BTC. Maximum one open position per asset prevents over-concentration. No naked exposure, ever.

DURATION
14–45 Day Expirations

Shorter durations accelerate time decay — the primary source of profit for call sellers — while maintaining enough distance to avoid gamma risk near expiry. The engine prefers USDC-settled linear options where available.

ROLL MECHANICS
Profit Capture Triggers New Cycle

When a position reaches its profit capture threshold, the engine buys back the current call and immediately sells a new one at a fresh strike. Locks in gains; resets the premium clock. Both legs verify before the journal updates.

RISK CONTROLS

Built for Correctness

5% OTM Floor

No strike closer than 5% above spot. Enforced at the timing engine and again at the screener. Strikes closer than the floor are filtered before they reach execution.

Fill Verification

Every order is polled for fill confirmation before the journal updates. Unfilled limits are canceled and retried as market orders. Failed market orders produce no journal entry — preventing the phantom-trade drift problem.

Bidirectional Reconciliation

Every cycle, the engine cross-checks its journal against Deribit's actual positions. Phantom journal entries are auto-canceled; orphan exchange positions are adopted into the journal. The bot and exchange never disagree about state.

Cash-Settled Assignments

If a call expires in-the-money, the position is cash-settled on Deribit — no physical delivery of coins. The engine calculates net P&L including assignment costs and logs the result.

One Position Per Asset

Hard cap — never more than one open position per asset. Prevents the strategy from doubling up on a single asset's expiration timeline.

30-Minute Cycle Discipline

All decisions happen on 30-minute boundaries. No reactive trading on price moves between cycles. The cadence prevents over-trading and reduces fee drag.

HONEST LIMITATIONS

What This Strategy Is Not

Not a directional bet. The strategy profits from time decay and volatility overpricing, not from predicting whether BTC goes up or down. In a strong bull market, call writing underperforms simply holding the asset.

Not a hedge. Selling calls generates income during flat or declining markets, but it doesn't protect against large drawdowns in the underlying. If BTC drops 30%, you still hold BTC.

Not high-frequency. The engine scans every 30 minutes, not every second. It competes on analysis quality and strike selection, not on speed.

Not unlimited capacity. The strategy requires holding the underlying asset to sell calls against. Income scales linearly with holdings, not exponentially.

Not foolproof. Assignment happens. Premiums vary. Some cycles produce no actionable signals. The edge is statistical — over many cycles, income from harvesting overpriced volatility exceeds the occasional cost of assignment. Any individual trade can lose.
