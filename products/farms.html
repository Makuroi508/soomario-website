THE EDGE

Most grids fail
because they're placed by guess

A grid earns when price oscillates and dies when price escapes. Place it at fixed percentages from spot and you're guessing where to be. Anchor it to the liquidation-zone topology of the asset and you're betting on a structural area where forced flow already concentrates. Farms places its grid where the leverage data says price has reasons to range — and runs an ATR trailing stop on a seed runner so when the range breaks upward, the strategy participates instead of getting capped.

HONEST LIMITS

What Farms isn't

Paper trading — not live capital

Live performance will differ from paper due to slippage, funding costs, and execution friction. Mainnet deployment is contingent on validation period completion.

Long-only by construction

An earlier bidirectional version was retired after a sharp pump in April 2026 produced compounded losses on the short side. The current design forgoes the ability to short on weakness — and forgoes the worst-case downside that pairs of opposing positions create. It's a deliberate trade-off, not a feature.

Sustained downtrends hurt

A long-only grid accumulating into a falling market is sitting on losing inventory. The drawdown halt — calculated on combined realised plus unrealised P&L — fires before the position becomes catastrophic, but it does fire. Farms is structurally built for ranging-to-rising regimes.

Statistical, not deterministic

Past paper-trading performance doesn't guarantee future results. The strategy depends on liquidation-zone ranges remaining stable enough to capture round-trips. Market structure changes can degrade the edge.
