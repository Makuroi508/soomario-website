THE EDGE

Sideways markets
are a profit center, not a problem

Most strategies need direction to make money. Range-bound markets — where price oscillates without committing to a trend — are precisely where they underperform. Farms inverts that: it positions for the round-trips that happen inside zones, capturing profit on both the bounce off the floor and the rejection off the ceiling. Volatility is the input, not the enemy.

HONEST LIMITS

What Farms isn't

Paper trading — not live capital

Live performance will differ from paper due to slippage, funding costs, and execution friction. Mainnet deployment is contingent on validation period completion.

Trending markets hurt range-farming

Strong directional moves break ranges and can cause repeated stop-outs. Farms is structurally a sideways-market strategy — when the market commits to a trend, the bot may sit out for extended periods or take losses on broken ranges.

Bidirectional means losses on both sides

Strong breakouts that invalidate the zone can hit both long and short positions in sequence. The exposure isn't fully hedged — it's range-dependent.

Statistical, not deterministic

Past paper-trading performance doesn't guarantee future results. The strategy depends on liquidation-zone ranges remaining stable enough to capture round-trips. Market structure changes can degrade the edge.
