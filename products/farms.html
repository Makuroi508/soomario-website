STRATEGY THESIS

A structural-flow strategy,
not a directional bet

Cryptocurrency perpetual futures markets exhibit a feature largely absent from spot markets: a continuously updating ledger of leveraged positions whose liquidation prices are publicly inferable from open-interest data. When price approaches a sufficiently dense cluster of liquidation levels, forced-flow events compress price toward that cluster, creating a brief window of mean-reverting behavior. Most retail trading systems are directional momentum strategies that attempt to predict the next leg of price action. Farms is not. It monetizes oscillation around well-defined liquidation anchors without taking a view on direction.

THE DISTINGUISHING FEATURE

Conviction weighting,
not uniform allocation

Rather than spreading capital uniformly across a static grid, Farms commits a measured opening position at the anchor, layers a patient buy grid beneath it, and escalates with a second tranche only when price moves decisively into the zone — adding size where the mean-reversion thesis is strongest and the entry is cheapest. The result is a position that is light when uncertain and heavy when conviction is earned by price.

Light when uncertain

The first tranche commits only ~40% of the farm's allocated margin. If price recovers from the entry shelf without triggering conviction escalation, exposure stays contained. Capital sits idle rather than chasing weak setups.

Heavy when earned

A second tranche deploys additional conviction size only when price moves decisively against the entry — and stays there. Size enters where the mean-reversion edge is statistically strongest, not where the original signal was weakest.

Patient by design

An empty deployment slate is acceptable. The selector ranks ~50 perpetuals every cycle and only opens new farms on assets ranked in the top 10 whose price has reached the entry shelf. Most cycles open zero farms.

THE TRANCHE LIFECYCLE

A deterministic state machine.
Capital committed only on confirmation.

1 · Entry shelf & seed

When a top-ranked candidate's price reaches its entry shelf at the long max-pain anchor, the farm opens its first tranche. The seed is a conviction-weighted market position — sized at 1.5× the farm's base unit — establishing immediate exposure at the anchor rather than waiting for a passive fill that may never come. The first tranche commits roughly 40% of the farm's allocated margin.

2 · The buy grid

Beneath the seed, the farm rests a grid of 8 limit buys spaced 0.5% apart. As price oscillates within the zone, fills lower the position's blended cost basis. Each filled level books a paired round-trip when price recovers — round-trips are the bulk of the trade count and the principal source of harvested profit.

3 · Tranche 2 escalation

The second tranche arms when the asset's new long max-pain prints at least 1% below the T1 entry's max-pain — and holds that level for at least 30 minutes. The sustain requirement filters transient wicks from genuine repricing. When T2 arms, additional conviction size deploys lower in the zone and the floor is armed (see below).

4 · Runner harvest

As price recovers from the average entry, the runner harvests progressively. The first take-profit closes 50% of the runner when price reaches halfway between the blended entry and the short max-pain level. The remaining half rides on a trailing stop that gives back 50% of the move from peak before exiting. Both legs use vault-truth position sizes so the farm exits truly flat.

5 · The floor (last resort)

A hard floor sits 5% below the lowest T2 level. The floor is armed only after T2 deploys — a T1-only farm has no floor. If price breaks the floor, the entire farm closes immediately. This protects against the failure mode where a position accumulates into a clean breakdown rather than a recoverable dislocation.

RANK ENGINE

Hysteresis prevents rank-wobble churn

Asymmetric band — open ≤10, hold ≤15

A new farm may only open on an asset ranked within the top 10. An existing farm is held until its asset falls below rank 15. The gap means an asset wobbling between ranks 11 and 14 is neither opened nor closed — it stays farmed if already farmed, or is passed over if not.

Time hysteresis — 45 min sustained

The first scan that finds a farm's rank past 15 does not close it — it marks the farm and starts a timer. Rotation fires only after the rank stays out-of-band for at least 45 minutes. With 30-min scans, that means two consecutive bad scans are required. A single transient dip costs nothing.

Force-include core

BTC, ETH, HYPE, SUI, XRP, SOL, ZEC are force-included — held regardless of rank, never marked for rotation. These are the platform's highest-conviction long-term anchors; they always have a slot when the strategy finds entry shelves on them.

The 7-component farmability score — price position (25%), zone width (20%), reversal setup (15%), volume (15%), open-interest depth (10%), range alignment (8%), L/S skew (7%) — feeds the rank. Assets failing a $100M open-interest filter are excluded from deployment regardless of score.

RISK FRAMEWORK

The vault is the source of truth

Continuous reconciliation

Every cycle, the strategy's internal position model is compared against the vault's actual holdings. If the vault shows less inventory than the model expects (phantom long), the model un-fills the most recent levels until it matches. If the vault shows more (oversized fill), the divergence is logged but the model doesn't act — it can't safely invent inventory. The vault is the single source of truth.

2× default leverage

Conservative by design. The conviction-weighting structure already concentrates capital where the entry is cheapest; layering high leverage on top would compound risk in the wrong direction. Hyperliquid's per-coin maximum leverage is respected; the bot does not exceed 2× regardless of what the venue allows.

Portfolio drawdown halts

Portfolio-level drawdown and daily-loss circuit breakers pause new farm deployment under stress. Existing farms continue to manage to their natural close — the halt blocks new exposure rather than forcing exits at unfavorable prices.

Vault-truth flatten

When any farm closes — runner trail, floor breach, rotation, or manual close — the bot sells broker.position_size(sym) rather than the model's recorded seed size. This ensures the farm exits truly flat even if the original market buy filled larger than modeled. A >5% divergence between vault size and model size emits a "flat-by-vault" warning.

HONEST LIMITS

What Farms isn't

Not a trend-follower

Farms is mean-reversion at the structural level. In strongly trending markets without zone touches, farms don't deploy. The selector ranks high, the entry shelves remain untouched, and capital waits. A bull market that runs in a straight line is not a market Farms harvests.

Not directional alpha

The strategy does not bet on price direction. It will not outperform a long-bias bot during a sustained directional move; it is built to outperform during the chop that typically follows or precedes those moves. The investor profile is "willing to be flat when the market is one-way."

Not unlimited capacity

Five concurrent farms across ~50 candidates is a deliberate cap. Increasing concurrency dilutes the per-farm allocation below the threshold where the tranche structure produces meaningful round-trip profit. Capacity scales with vault size up to the point where per-farm position sizes start to slip on Hyperliquid's order book.

Leveraged perpetuals carry liquidation risk

Farms uses 2× default leverage with vault-truth reconciliation and per-tranche floors. The position-management logic significantly reduces tail risk, but Hyperliquid's standard liquidation mechanics still apply at the position level in extreme moves. The full deposit is at risk of loss.
