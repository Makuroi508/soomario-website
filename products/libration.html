THE EDGE

Volatility overreacts.
Libration is built to be patient.

Volatile perpetual markets spend a lot of time overreacting. A sharp, panicked flush frequently overshoots fair value and snaps back; a blow-off in strength frequently exhausts and rolls over. Libration expresses a single frozen thesis — statistically oversold and exhausted conditions on multi-hour timeframes tend to mean-revert. It waits for that condition to appear on a completed bar, takes one position in the indicated direction, and lets a trailing stop harvest the move.

WHAT YOU GET

One shared book. Self-custody.

Broad volatile basket

Libration tracks and trades roughly twenty-one high-volatility perpetual markets, taking both long oversold-reversal setups and short exhaustion setups. It is directional and opportunistic across the whole basket rather than concentrated in any one name.

Layered protection

Every position gets a hard, exchange-resting protective stop the instant it opens, plus a self-tightening trailing stop that ratchets in the favorable direction only. A daily-drawdown halt stops new risk-taking on bad days.

On-chain, self-custody

The whole book runs from one shared Hyperliquid vault under isolated margin per position. Funds stay on-chain in the vault contract; the bot holds only an API signing key with trading permissions, never withdrawal control.

HOW IT WORKS

Mechanical, narrow, capital-conservative

Completed bars only

Entries are evaluated exclusively on the close of a completed multi-hour bar, never on a partially formed one. A dedicated guard ensures each bar is evaluated exactly once, so a single condition can never fire an entry twice, and live behavior stays faithful to how the strategy was validated.

One position per coin

On a valid signal Libration opens a single position — long on an oversold-reversal, short on an exhaustion setup — and will not add to it. No pyramiding, no averaging down. The position lives until its exit logic closes it.

Sizing that compounds with the book

Position size is a fixed, conservative fraction of current account equity, recomputed at entry off live equity. Sizing scales up as the account grows and down as it shrinks, with no manual intervention. Newer, unproven names are sized down further.

Exchange-is-truth reconciliation

Libration treats the exchange as the source of truth for what is open. It only records a position as closed on confirmed data, re-adopts and re-protects any position it finds untracked, and escalates an unresolvable divergence to a human rather than guessing.

HONEST LIMITS

What Libration isn't

A shared, leveraged book

All positions run from one account. In a synchronized market selloff, many correlated positions can stop out in the same session. The daily-drawdown halt caps new risk-taking, but it does not stop losses accruing on positions already open.

The universe is a deployment choice

Libration currently trades roughly twenty-one volatile perpetual markets, but the exact roster is set at deployment time and can change. Any specific count describes a particular deployment, not an immutable feature of the strategy.

Exit protection is polling-dependent

Between polling cycles, a very fast price gap could fill a stop at a worse level than intended. On some shared-account configurations native exchange stops may be less reliable, in which case the in-process backstop becomes the primary line of defense — and it acts only as often as the polling cycle runs.

In live validation

Libration is in a controlled live trial at low leverage on a small account, measuring whether real-world execution friction stays small enough to preserve the historical edge. We do not publish returns, win rates, or drawdown statistics we cannot yet stand behind with live, friction-inclusive data.
