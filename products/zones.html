THE STRUCTURAL EDGE

Liquidation data is public.
Systematic execution is rare.

Liquidation cascades happen on every major derivatives exchange and the data is observable in real time. Anyone with a Coinglass account can see where leveraged positions cluster and where the next forced-selling event would land. The information asymmetry isn't in the data — it's in execution.

Most retail traders see a cascade after it has already happened and react emotionally. Most discretionary traders pick one coin, build one thesis, and miss the parallel setups across 20 other coins. Most algorithmic strategies optimize on chart patterns and ignore the venue-specific microstructure where actual forced selling occurs.

Zones treats liquidation proximity as a portfolio-wide statistical signal across 21 coins. The engine doesn't act on every zone touch — it requires its DCA scoring AND an independent conviction filter to clear before deploying capital. In practice, most zone touches across the universe are ignored. When the engine does enter, the dislocation is large enough, the OI fuel is deep enough, and broader conditions support the snap-back.

Across 21 coins, the strategy typically holds three to six concurrent positions and turns over capital on a 12–48 hour cycle. The combination of selectivity and the DCA structure — which mechanically improves average entry as dislocation deepens — is what turns a public observation into a tradeable edge.

SIGNAL ARCHITECTURE

Two zones per coin.
One conviction gate.

The engine continuously ranks all 21 coins by DCA opportunity quality and only commits capital to the highest-ranked candidates that also clear an independent conviction filter. Zone scanning, conviction signaling, and position management run as decoupled loops with independent state.

STAGE 1
Cross-Exchange Zone Aggregation

Open interest, liquidation density, and 24-hour ranges are pulled from Binance, Bybit, and OKX every cycle. Two max-pain zones are computed per coin: a Long Max Pain band below price (where leveraged longs would be wiped in a downside cascade) and a Short Max Pain band above (where leveraged shorts would be squeezed in an upside cascade). The zones come from observable market microstructure — not chart patterns.

STAGE 2
DCA Score Ranking

Each coin gets a composite score from three observable variables: zone width (wider zones mean larger potential dislocations), proximity (how close current price is to triggering an entry edge), and open interest depth (more OI means more liquidation fuel). Across the 21-coin universe, only top-ranked candidates are eligible for entry consideration.

STAGE 3
Conviction Gate

Before any capital deploys, the candidate must independently clear a conviction filter combining RSI extremes, volume anomalies, longs/shorts imbalance, market-wide Fear & Greed, and trend alignment. The gate also resolves direction: downside-favorable conditions open long; upside-favorable conditions open short. The gate exists specifically to prevent blindly buying every zone.

SELECTIVITY IS THE POINT

Across 21 actively scanned coins, the engine typically opens between zero and four positions per scoring cycle. Most cycles open none. The exact weights, scoring thresholds, and conviction parameters are calibrated against historical data and not published.

POSITION CONSTRUCTION

Stack into the dislocation.
Scale out as it normalizes.

STEP 1
4-Level DCA Stack

When a coin clears both ranking and conviction, capital deploys across four graduated price levels within the liquidation zone — one immediate entry at the zone edge, three resting limit orders at progressively deeper prices. Allocations are weighted toward the deeper levels because deeper fills represent better entry prices.

STEP 2
Tiered Leverage

Each coin's leverage is calibrated to its historical volatility band, capped at 8×. Higher-volatility coins trade at the lower end of the leverage range; lower-volatility coins at the higher end. Uniform leverage across heterogeneous volatility profiles either over-risks the volatile coins or under-uses capital on the stable ones.

STEP 3
Dynamic Take-Profit

Three profit targets close portions of the position as the market reverts, with a trailing-stop runner protecting the remainder. Targets are computed from the position's actual average entry — as deeper DCA levels fill and average entry improves, profit targets move closer in price terms. The math rewards averaging into the dislocation.

The same logic applies to both directions: long entries average down as price falls into the long-side zone; short entries average up as price rises into the short-side zone. Bi-directional by construction, not by overlay.

RISK MANAGEMENT

Per-position stops.
Portfolio-level circuit breaker.

Per-Level Stop Loss

Every position carries a hard stop at a fixed buffer beyond the deepest DCA level. The buffer accounts for typical wick noise without exposing the position to a thesis breakdown. Enforced bot-side every cycle and re-armed whenever fill state changes.

Catastrophic Stop

A second, wider stop sits at a multiple of the per-level distance, intended to fire only in true black-swan conditions where the per-level stop would have been wicked but missed. No single position can take the portfolio down beyond a bounded amount, regardless of how the standard stop fails.

State Integrity

Internal position state is reconciled against three independent sources every cycle: Hyperliquid's clearinghouse state, user fills history, and the local positions file. Discrepancies — orphan limit orders, manually closed positions, ghost state — are detected and resolved automatically.

48-Hour Time Exit

Every position has an unconditional 48-hour time exit. A thesis that hasn't played out in 48 hours is statistically less likely to play out at all, and locked capital is dead capital. The time exit closes the position at market regardless of P&L, freeing the slot for a fresher opportunity.

Portfolio Circuit Breaker

Once trailing drawdown crosses a defined threshold, the breaker pauses all new entries. Existing positions continue to manage normally. The breaker resets when equity recovers above the prior peak.

Equity Sanity Filter

Equity readings outside a sanity band (for instance, a one-cycle drop to near-zero indicating a stale API response) are flagged as glitches and excluded from the equity log without halting trading. The bug that kills you is rarely the strategy bug — it's the bug where the bot thinks state has changed when it hasn't.

EDGE THESIS

Why this works
when others don't

Liquidation data is public — execution is rare

Anyone can see where leveraged positions cluster. Almost nobody systematically positions for the snap-back across 21 coins in parallel. The infrastructure to track 21 markets continuously, rank them by opportunity quality, and execute against them under disciplined rules — that's the moat.

Multi-signal confirmation prevents zone trap

DCA scoring gets a coin onto the radar. Conviction has to independently clear the gate before any capital deploys. A zone touch alone never opens a position. False starts are cheap; bad fills are expensive.

DCA structure mechanically improves entries

The deeper the dislocation, the more the average entry improves. Even adverse scenarios that fill all four levels still produce better cost bases than single-shot entries would have. The structure rewards averaging into the move, not flinching.

Dynamic take-profits track real cost basis

Targets move with the actual average entry, not the original zone midpoint. Improved fills make targets reachable instead of stranding the position waiting for a snap-back to a price the strategy never paid.

Discipline beats novelty

Liquidation tracking, DCA averaging, conviction filters, dynamic take-profits — none of these are individually novel. What's rare is applying them with the discipline of a portfolio-wide automated system that runs every five minutes for months without missing cycles.

HONEST LIMITS

What Zones isn't

Not a trend-follower

Zones is mean-reversion. It enters when prices have dislocated from fair value and exits when they reconverge. In sustained directional markets without liquidation cascades, the engine simply sits on its hands.

Not an alpha generator across all market conditions

The edge depends on liquidation cascades happening with reasonable frequency. In long quiet markets with low leverage and tight ranges, opportunities thin out and the engine produces less.

Not unlimited capacity

The strategy operates on Hyperliquid order book depth. There's a real notional capacity ceiling per coin before slippage degrades fills. Capacity scales with HL's overall liquidity, not infinitely.

Not foolproof

Drawdowns happen. Stops get hit. Some theses don't play out. The edge is statistical, not deterministic. Past performance doesn't guarantee anything; it documents what the strategy has done, not what it will do.
