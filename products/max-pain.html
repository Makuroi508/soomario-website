THE THESIS

Price Travels to Pain

"Max pain" originally described the price level at which the maximum number of options contracts expire worthless — a level markets have a curious habit of drifting toward near expiry. The strategy generalizes that idea to perpetual futures: look for price levels where forced liquidations from over-leveraged positions create mechanical reversal pressure, and trade into them.

Retail traders in crypto perps fight a structural disadvantage. Professional firms deploy quant research teams, risk managers, and millions in infrastructure. Individuals typically compete with a charting app and willpower. Studies show median retail account lifespans under 90 days. The gap isn't intelligence — it's tooling, discipline, and execution quality.

Max Pain Vault closes that gap by combining two tools that are both strong on their own and stronger together: TradingView's charting engine for signal generation, and a custom Python bot for fast execution and independent risk checks. Neither can do the job alone. Together they remove the three failure modes that kill most retail trading: signal inconsistency, emotional exits, and execution latency.

ARCHITECTURE

Three Layers, No Single Point of Failure

Each layer solves a specific problem, and the layers are independent enough that a failure in one doesn't cascade.

1. Signal Generation · TradingView

Each of the 18 coins runs the Soomario Max Pain v3.2 BALANCED pine script on its own chart. The strategy evaluates three conditions on every candle close: an RSI crossover on per-coin thresholds, a volume-weighted Liquidation Z-Score spike, and a Supertrend flip. All three must align for a signal to fire.

2. Execution · Railway Bot

When TradingView alerts, it webhooks a Python Flask service on Railway. The bot validates the signal, sets the tier-appropriate leverage, and market-opens the position on Hyperliquid via the official SDK. End-to-end latency from webhook to on-chain confirmation averages 800 milliseconds. If an opposing signal arrives on a coin with an existing position, the bot closes and flips automatically.

3. Safety · Independent 45s Stop-Loss

TradingView only evaluates exits on candle close — up to 59 minutes between checks on 1H. The bot's safety loop polls Hyperliquid every 45 seconds, computes unrealized P&L on every open position, and force-closes anything past its hard stop-loss threshold without waiting for a pine-side signal.

ANTI-FRAGILITY PRINCIPLE

TradingView offline? The bot still enforces hard stops. Railway offline? TradingView still fires close signals on candle. Hyperliquid API flaky? The bot retries with backoff. Redundancy is the risk management.

SIGNAL DISCIPLINE

Three Indicators, All Must Agree

Any one of these alone throws too many false positives. Requiring all three cuts the trade count but drives the win rate toward 82%.

INDICATOR 1
RSI

Momentum on a 0–100 scale. Per-coin tuned length and thresholds because different assets have different momentum structures — PUMP uses length 5 for responsiveness; TAO uses length 2 for high-volatility extremes.

INDICATOR 2
Liquidation Z-Score

Custom indicator measuring how unusual the current candle's volume-weighted move is versus recent history. Z=3 is three standard deviations — statistically rare, typically a forced-liquidation event. Spikes combined with RSI extremes mark local capitulation.

INDICATOR 3
Supertrend

Volatility-adaptive trend indicator with dynamic support/resistance lines that flip decisively on trend changes. Flips confirm the reversal that RSI and Z-Score are hinting at. Per-coin period and multiplier, always.

RISK CONTROLS

The Safety Net in Full

Per-Coin Hard Stop

−6% to −12.5% thresholds, backtested per coin. Enforced by the bot every 45 seconds regardless of TradingView state.

3× Max Leverage

Cap applies universally. Tier 3 coins further limited to 2×. No leverage knob in the wrong hands.

No Leverage Stacking

Bot refuses duplicate same-direction entries. A second long signal on an already-long coin is logged, not executed.

Auto-Flip Opposite Signals

When an opposing signal arrives on an open position, the bot closes and flips atomically — never holds against an active signal.

3× Retry on API Errors

Hyperliquid 5xx on update_leverage triggers exponential backoff retry. Real failures alert Telegram; transient failures self-resolve.

Public Health Endpoint

Railway bot exposes uptime, last scan time, and webhook status publicly. Anyone can verify the bot is alive and processing.

WHY VAULT

Max Pain Vault charges one fee: 10% of net profits. No deposit fees, no withdrawal fees, no monthly management fees, no lockups. If the vault loses money, the operator earns nothing until it recovers to its previous high-water mark.

Traditional hedge funds charge "2 and 20" — 2% of assets annually plus 20% of profits — which means investors pay in losing years. A pure profit-share structure only earns when depositors earn. That's the alignment.

Hyperliquid's native vault infrastructure keeps custody in the smart contract. The operator can direct trading but cannot withdraw depositor funds. Withdrawals are one-click and on-chain. Minimum deposit is $10.

RISK DISCLOSURE

Trading leveraged crypto perpetuals is substantially risky. Up to 3× leverage amplifies both gains and losses. A worst-case simultaneous hard-stop event across all 18 coins would produce roughly 25% vault drawdown — survivable, but real.

Smart contracts carry inherent risk of bugs, exploits, or unforeseen interactions. The strategy depends on TradingView, Railway, and Hyperliquid all functioning; extended outages at any of these can widen losses beyond backtest expectations. Market dynamics change over time — a strategy that worked across 2023–2026 is not guaranteed to continue working. Backtest and Monte Carlo results are historical; they are not predictions. Never deposit funds you cannot afford to lose.
