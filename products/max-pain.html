THE EDGE

Liquidation Max Pain
is a real anchor

Most retail DCA strategies build their ladders on fixed percentages — buy every 2% down, every $1 of price. This treats price as homogeneous, which it is not. In leveraged perpetual markets, price moves through clusters of stop orders and liquidation triggers that are geographically dense around specific levels. When price arrives at such a cluster, forced selling — or forced buying — accelerates the move and reliably overshoots the cluster's center before reversing. Liquidation Max Pain, computable in real time from aggregated open interest across Binance, Bybit, and OKX, is the closest publicly observable proxy for where leveraged positioning will be forcefully unwound. Empirically, prices revisit the Long Max Pain level during pullbacks more often than the surrounding round numbers, support and resistance levels, or moving averages — and the bounces from these visits are more violent than average.

ZONE CONSTRUCTION

Bands that adapt
to each asset's volatility

For each asset, the engine pulls aggregated open interest from Binance, Bybit, and OKX, computes a volatility scalar, and derives a four-level zone band around each Max Pain. Zones refresh hourly. The bot deploys at only two of the four levels — the rest serve scoring and analytical roles.

L1 — market entry, 30% of notional

When a candidate clears both the conviction filter and the 2.0% proximity gate, the bot fires a market order at current price sized to 30% of the position's intended notional. This establishes the position immediately at the zone edge — no waiting for a passive fill that may never come.

L4 — resting limit, 70% of notional

The remaining 70% rests as a post-only limit at the deepest zone level, roughly two volatility scalars below Max Pain. If price falls into L4, average entry adjusts downward into the cluster the strategy is designed to capture. If price recovers from L1 without reaching L4, the position runs with reduced exposure.

Atomic batch submission

Both orders are submitted as a single atomic batch to Hyperliquid, hitting the order book in the same block. Never with one resting while the other is still in flight. This eliminates the failure mode where partial submission leaves the position in an ambiguous state.

DESIGN ITERATION

Why two levels,
not four

The strategy's initial design used four levels — L1, L2, L3, L4. Two waves of structural simplification have since produced the current two-level configuration. Both were data-driven.

L3 retired first (v1.0)

Analysis of historical deployments showed positions that filled L1, L2, and L3 but never L4 lost a mean of $10.12 across 22 trades, while L4-included positions earned a mean of $2.22. L3 was a structural trap — committing meaningful capital but not enough to be priced for the reversion the strategy is built to capture. Retired, capital redistributed to surviving levels.

L2 retired in v2.0 (June 2026)

A subsequent review of the L1/L2/L4 configuration produced a similar conclusion about the intermediate L2. Partially-filled L1+L2-without-L4 positions consistently underperformed their L4-completing counterparts. The L2 leg added ambiguity to sizing without materially improving fill quality. Retired, capital redistributed into a clean 30/70 split between L1 and L4.

Cleaner state, fewer transitions

The current structure preserves the strategy's defining behavior — capture the bounce off the deepest liquidation level — while removing the noise that intermediate ladder rungs introduced. Operationally: two atomic orders instead of three, fewer partial-fill state transitions to reconcile, and a cleaner position-card lifecycle in the operator dashboard.

EXIT STACK

Three closes, in sequence

Midpoint TP — primary close

When price reaches the geometric center between Long Max Pain and Short Max Pain, the bot closes 50% of the position's total size. The midpoint is computed at deploy time from the zone data captured on the entry and locked for the position's life — intermediate zone refreshes do not reprice it. The midpoint is the equilibrium price between bull and bear liquidation pressure, and taking profit there banks the cleanest segment of the expected mean-reversion move.

Retrace trail — runs the remainder

After the midpoint fires, the remaining 50% runs on a retrace trail. The trail tracks the highest price seen since entry, exiting when price retraces 50% of the move from average entry to the running peak. Entry $100, peak $120 → trail exits at $110. Half the move ceded back, the other half realized when the stop fires. The trail arms only after the midpoint fires — before that, the position is protected only by the catastrophic stop.

Catastrophic SL — backstop

10% beyond the deepest zone level (L4). For longs, that places the stop 10% below L4; for shorts, 10% above the upper L4. The widened buffer — increased from 5% in v1.0 — gives the L4 limit room to fill and the position room to breathe before forced exit. When price crosses, the bot fires an IOC market-close on all remaining size.

Time exit — disabled in v2.0

A 48-hour time exit that force-closes any position older than two days remains implemented but disabled. Historical analysis showed time-exit closes were nearly always P&L-positive but consistently truncated otherwise-running winners. The feature was retired pending further validation. Positions now run until one of midpoint TP, retrace trail, or catastrophic SL fires.

ACCOUNT-LEVEL SAFEGUARDS

Five layers of risk control

$200M OI gate (new in v2.0)

Before a coin is considered deployable, its aggregated open interest across Binance, Bybit, and OKX must equal or exceed $200 million. Max Pain calculations on thin markets are statistically noisy and operationally manipulable — a $50M-OI coin doesn't have a stable enough liquidation distribution to anchor a position on. Coins that fall below silently exit ranking until they re-qualify.

15% drawdown circuit breaker

The bot tracks rolling equity and peak equity continuously. If drawdown crosses 15% from peak, new entries stop while existing positions continue to manage to their natural close. The breaker resets only when equity recovers above the prior peak. In production, the breaker has not tripped — selectivity and tight per-position SL keep account-level drawdown well below the threshold.

Stale-order cleanup (24h)

Any L4 limit resting more than 24 hours on a position whose L1 never filled, or whose original conviction-and-proximity setup has expired, is automatically cancelled. This prevents abandoned limits from accumulating on the order book and triggering surprise fills when price revisits old zones days later.

Orphan-order sweep

Every cycle, the bot enumerates all open orders on the vault and cancels any that don't correspond to a tracked position record. This catches orders left behind by partial restart failures and ensures position state stays synchronized with exchange state.

Dead-man's switch (pending)

A server-side dead-man's switch via Hyperliquid's scheduleCancel mechanism is implemented in the bot. Activation is gated on the vault crossing Hyperliquid's $1M cumulative volume threshold; the bot detects the rejection on first attempt, self-disables for the remainder of the run, and re-tests on each deploy. Once active, it fires on the exchange side if the bot itself goes offline for more than 10 minutes.

Hourly zone stability

Zones refresh on a one-hour cadence. Intermediate price moves do not trigger recomputation. This is intentional: zone stability over a position's life prevents the bot from chasing its own placed orders as price oscillates within a band, and preserves the integrity of the catastrophic stop level the bot committed to at deploy time.

HONEST LIMITS

What Max Pain isn't

Not a trend-follower

Max Pain is mean-reversion. It enters where prices have dislocated from fair value and exits when they reconverge. In sustained directional markets without liquidation cascades — the slow grind regimes — the engine simply sits on its hands. Capital remains in the vault rather than being deployed into low-probability setups.

Not alpha in every regime

The edge depends on liquidation cascades happening with reasonable frequency on assets that clear the $200M OI gate. In long quiet markets with low leverage and tight ranges, the conviction gate rejects most candidates and opportunities thin out. The strategy is profitable across the full data window but does not outperform every week.

Not unlimited capacity

The strategy operates on Hyperliquid order book depth. There's a real notional capacity ceiling per coin before slippage degrades fills. The vault's current scale runs well within capacity; scaling beyond it would require either fragmenting orders across additional time slices or capping deposits.

Leveraged perpetuals carry liquidation risk

Max Pain trades leveraged perpetual futures. The per-position SL and catastrophic stop reduce tail risk, but Hyperliquid's standard liquidation mechanics still apply at the position level. The full deposit is at risk of loss.
