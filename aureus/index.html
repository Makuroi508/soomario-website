NAV YOU CAN VERIFY

A hard floor, computed on-chain

AUREUS publishes a "hard floor" NAV computed entirely from on-chain reads — no operator trust required. It sums the USDC across four tracked addresses plus pending Hyperliquid bridge withdrawals, divided by AUR supply.

A separate soft layer reports live trading state, capped at 10× hard reserves to prevent misreports. Both views are surfaced in the dashboard.

hardReserves = Σ USDC across 4 tracked addresses + pending HL bridge withdrawals hardNav = hardReserves / totalSupply(AUR)

OPERATOR AUTHORITY

What the multisig can and cannot do

The multisig CAN
  • Unpause / pause
  • Close epochs (within 15% rate limit)
  • Process redemptions
  • Sweep capital to trading
  • Configure tracked addresses (24h timelock)
The multisig CANNOT
  • Touch your AUR balance
  • Change the 80/5/5/10 split
  • Move NAV more than 15% per close
  • Take more than its 5% (or 15% post-Recovery)
  • Mint AUR without depositing matching USDC

Every operator action runs through an audited admin dashboard that submits to the multisig queue for 2-of-3 signing — the encoded transaction is reviewable before any signature is collected. All actions are public on Arbiscan in real time.

Risks

  • Smart-contract risk. Internal red-team passed three times. External audit deferred to Months 2–6. The 90-day caps mitigate aggregate exposure.
  • Operator key risk. 2-of-3 Safe at launch; moving to 2-of-4 in the first 30 days.
  • Trading-strategy risk. Profits are not guaranteed; epochs can close flat or negative.
  • Hyperliquid platform risk. Trading executes on Hyperliquid; HL outages, bugs, or insolvency would affect reserves.
  • Bridge risk. Capital in transit between Arbitrum and Hyperliquid is exposed to bridge-contract risk.
  • Strategy concentration risk. The underlying strategies share venue (Hyperliquid) and broadly correlated asset universes. Diversification across strategies reduces single-strategy risk but does not eliminate correlated drawdowns in adverse market regimes.

This is not financial advice. Deposit only what you can afford to lose.
